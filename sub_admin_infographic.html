Feature Architecture Proposal

WorkSync Sub-Admin Privileges

Transitioning from an "All-or-Nothing" access model to a sophisticated, modular access control system with 21 granular permissions.

The Current Bottle-Neck

Currently, WorkSync evaluates admin access using a single "Master Key" approach:

Imagine giving your new Shift Manager the keys to the front door so they can manage the weekly schedule.

But because there's only one "Master Key", you accidentally also gave them the combination to the office safe (Payroll), the authority to permanently fire staff, and access to change company-wide system settings.

This creates a major security risk: When you hire an assistant strictly to help with Scheduling or Leave Approvals, they automatically receive access to your Payroll Multipliers, Terminating Accounts, and advanced Notifications.

The "All-or-Nothing" Door
Dashboard Payroll Multipliers Schedule Terminate Accounts Leave Approvals System Webhooks

The Solution: Specific Permissions

We can't just separate everything randomly, because certain pages depend on others to work properly. Below is how we will intelligently link permissions together for a smooth experience.

The "Universal Background Check"

View Employee List
The ability to see who works here.

Scheduling

A manager needs to see the staff list before they can assign them a shift.

Leave Approvals

A manager needs to see the staff list to look up their current leave balances.

Notification Logs

A manager needs the list to filter alerts by specific employees.

Status Dashboard

You must literally see the list to know who checked in.

💡 Auto-Grant Rule: To make your life easier, if you give a Sub-Admin the "Scheduling" privilege, the system will automatically let them view the employee list. No need for you to check multiple boxes to get one job done!

Quarantining High-Risk Actions

Certain actions are too destructive to be bundled inside standard management tabs. We are carving these out into entirely separate switches. This way you can let a manager edit profiles without accidentally giving them the power to delete staff members permanently.

Delete Accounts

Complete erasure of an employee record. This power is cut out from general "Employee Management" to prevent accidental data loss.

Force Clock-Out

Manually clocking someone out of their shift. Even if an assistant can view the entire dashboard, they cannot interfere with active shifts unless you allow it.

Edit Timesheets

Changing past clock-in and clock-out periods which directly alters payroll. Isolated heavily to avoid unauthorized tampering.

The 5-Step Implementation Plan

1

Database Foundations

We will update the system's core database so every employee profile can securely store a personalized checklist of exactly what actions they're permitted to do, separating "Limited Sub-Admins" from full "Master Admins."

2

Security Pass Verification

Instead of just broadly asking "Is this person an admin?", the system's login process will now read their digital ID badge and securely remember their personalized checklist anywhere they go within the app.

3

Invisible Doors (Navigation)

We will update the application's layout so that restricted pages literally disappear from their screen. If a Sub-Admin doesn't have access to "Payroll Settings", the Settings tab will not show up on their menu.

4

Smart Buttons

We will trace through every single screen and make sure dangerous buttons (like "Delete Employee") magically hide themselves if the logged-in user doesn't have the specific clearance to use them.

5

The Management Control Panel

Finally, we will build a sleek, point-and-click interface for you, the business owner. When creating a Sub-Admin, you will see a list of 21 simple toggles. Check exactly what you want them to do, and the system seamlessly handles the rest!
